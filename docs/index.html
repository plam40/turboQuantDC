7 Research Breakthroughs

Each independently proven. Together, they define a new frontier.

#1

Mean-Removed Quantization

One line. +25% quality.
# The most impactful line of code in the entire codebase
keys -= keys.mean(dim=seq, keepdim=True)

Per-head key mean carries 57% of total variance but is completely invisible to attention (softmax is shift-invariant). Standard quantization wastes its codebook encoding something the model never uses. Removing the mean before quantization gives a free +1 bit of effective precision. Mean-removed 3-bit beats standard 4-bit on every metric.

3-bit generation: token match 26% → 64%. First divergence: token 50 → token 128.

Validated on Qwen 3B, 14B, 32B, 72B
#2

PCA-Adaptive Rotation

13x lower MSE

Everyone uses random rotation (WHT). We tried PCA. 48.7% of KV variance lives in 10% of coordinates. WHT ignores this. PCA exploits it. Calibrate on 128 tokens, transfers perfectly across prompts.

Top-5 attention match: 94.4% → 100% at 3-bit.

Compute once per model. Use forever.
#3

Asymptotic Compression Law

O(1/n) bits/token

The longer the context, the better the compression. Gini coefficient rises from 0.60 at 128 tokens to 0.85 at 2K. At 2K, only 0.3% of tokens get meaningful attention. Theoretical minimum: 0.015 bits/token.

This inverts "longer context = more memory."

Fundamental information-theoretic result
#4

ResidualQuant > QJL

+19% attention match

The paper's QJL (random Gaussian projection) is unbiased but high-variance. Our ResidualQuant stores sign(r_rotated) directly. Lower variance beats unbiasedness for autoregressive generation.

"we killed QJL early for the same reason. the stacking numbers speak for themselves."
— Tom Turney, TurboQuant author
Independently confirmed
#5

DeltaQuant

6.1x at 3-bit quality

Group similar tokens. Encode anchors at 3-bit, deltas at 1-2 bit. No error accumulation — each token independently references its anchor. Delta variance ratio: 0.248 (deltas are 4x tighter).

At 1-bit deltas: 8.5x compression beating uniform 2-bit.

Novel: cross-token deltas in WHT space
#6

FAISS Retrieval Attention

27x faster at 65K

Why compute attention over 1M tokens when only 128 matter? k=128 + window=64 gives 0.99 cosine to full attention. At 1M context: 5,208x fewer ops. Combined with 3-bit compression: 7.5x memory reduction.

At 1M context: 4.6 GB instead of 34.3 GB.

O(log n) attention
#7

29x CUDA Speedup at d=256

Triton's hidden cliff

Triton's register allocator overflows at d=256 (256x256 matmul tile). Our raw CUDA kernel avoids this entirely. WHT extended to d=2048. Directly benefits Gemma 4 (d=256 heads).

What Failed (Equally Valuable)

Honest science. 4 dead ends, published with evidence.

Cross-Head Delta

GQA heads are deliberately specialized (cosine=0.12). Deltas are 1.41x LARGER than absolutes. Dead on arrival for modern architectures.

Cross-Layer Prediction

Each layer's KV cache is independent. Deltas 2.6x larger, zero correlation. No free lunch from cross-layer redundancy.

Temporal Delta Coding

Keys ARE correlated (cosine=0.80) but O(sqrt(T)) error accumulation kills it. Anchored coding eats the compression advantage.

Spectral/DCT Compression

DCT energy is flat across KV coefficients (75% needed for 90% energy). WHT rotation already decorrelates optimally. KLT works but needs calibration.
